## 📋 PRE-WORKOUT CHECKLIST (every session)
1. **Hydration:** Drink 500ml water 30 min before. Carry 1L bottle to gym. Target 3L+ across the day.
2. **Joint check:** Press L ankle and R knee — pain >3/10? → switch to **FLARE PROTOCOL** (below).

4. **Fuel:** Light carb + protein 60–90 min prior (e.g., banana + Greek yogurt; oatmeal + whey).

6. **BP check (weekly, Day 1):** If resting BP >160/100 or <90/60 → skip weights, do gentle walk only.
7. **RPE plan:** Review today's target RPE before starting.

## 🧊 POST-WORKOUT CHECKLIST
1. **Rehydrate:** 500–750ml water within 30 min; add electrolytes if sweated heavily.
2. **Protein:** 30–40g within 60 min (whey shake, chicken, eggs, cottage cheese, tofu).
3. **Foam roll (5 min):** Quads, IT band, upper back, lats — 30 sec/area, avoid rolling directly over joints.
4. **Static stretch:** See cool-down routine.
5. **Joint scan:** Any swelling/warmth in ankle or knee? Ice 15 min if suspicious.
6. **Log it:** Weight used, reps completed, RPE, joint feedback. Progression depends on this.
7. **Sleep target:** 7–8 hr. Recovery = fat loss.

# 🚨 GOUT FLARE PROTOCOL

**Definition of flare:** Sudden joint pain, redness, warmth, or swelling in ankle/knee/foot, OR pain >4/10 at rest.

### Day-of flare:
1. **DO NOT train the affected joint or load-bear.**
2. Hydrate aggressively: **4 L water minimum**, low-purine diet today (avoid red meat, shellfish, beer).
3. Contact GP if first flare in months or pain >7/10. Take prescribed flare meds (e.g., colchicine/NSAID) per doctor.
4. Ice 15 min × 3 times that day.

### Replacement workouts during flare (until pain returns to baseline, usually 3–10 days):

**REPLACEMENT DAY A (was Day 1 or 3 — Upper):**
- All upper body work as planned, but **fully seated** versions only
- Skip standing Pallof, farmer's carry, any standing
- Substitute: **seated cable Pallof (kneeling on pad)**, **seated DB shoulder press**
- Cardio: 20 min **arm ergometer** OR upper-body bike if available, RPE 4

**REPLACEMENT DAY B (was Day 2 or 5 — Lower/Cardio):**
- **Skip all lower body resistance**
- Do **upper body day** instead, OR rest
- Cardio: **upper-body ergometer** 25 min RPE 4, OR swimming/pool walking if accessible (best gout-friendly option)
- Core only: dead bug, bird dog (forearms, knee on pad), supine marches

**REPLACEMENT DAY C (was Day 4 — Recovery):**
- Keep mobility but **eliminate any ankle/knee loading**
- Bike replaced with arm ergometer or skip
- Foam roll upper body only
- Add extra hydration & breathing work

### Return-to-training criteria after flare:
- Pain ≤2/10 at rest for 48 hr
- Full pain-free range of motion in affected joint
- Restart at **70% of pre-flare weights**, 1 set fewer, build back over 5–7 sessions

# ⚠️ RED FLAGS — STOP & SEEK MEDICAL ATTENTION
- Chest pain, pressure, or unusual shortness of breath
- Dizziness, lightheadedness, or near-fainting (nebivolol can cause this — don't push through)
- Sudden severe joint swelling
- Calf pain/swelling (DVT risk in obesity)
- BP at rest >180/110 or <90/60 with symptoms
- Resting RPE >3 ("I feel exhausted at rest") — take a rest day
